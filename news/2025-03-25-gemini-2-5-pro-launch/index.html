Google DeepMind Releases Gemini 2.5 Pro With Top Coding and Reasoning Scores

Google DeepMind released Gemini 2.5 Pro in experimental preview on March 25 2025, posting the highest score on LMArena and top results on GPQA Diamond and AIME 2025 -- making it the strongest reasoning model Google had shipped to date.

Gemini 2.5 Pro debuted as the highest-rated model on LMArena's human-preference leaderboard and claimed first place on GPQA Diamond (graduate-level science reasoning) and AIME 2025 (competitive math). For developers who benchmark on SWE-bench Verified, it landed above Claude 3.7 Sonnet and GPT-4o at the time of release. The experimental preview was immediately available to Gemini Advanced subscribers and through the Gemini API for developers. At $20 per month, Gemini Advanced now pairs the model with Google Workspace integration -- a pricing angle that is harder to ignore for teams already in the Google ecosystem. The honest caveat is that leaderboard rankings and real coding assistance are different things: devs switching from GitHub Copilot or Cursor should run a week-long trial on a production codebase before committing, since benchmark gaps rarely translate cleanly to daily refactors.
