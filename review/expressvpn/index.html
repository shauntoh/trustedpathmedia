Pros & Cons

What works

  • Lightspeed protocol delivers fast, consistent speeds particularly on long-distance connections
  • Servers in 105+ countries — one of the broadest geographic coverages of any major VPN
  • Polished, consistent apps across all major platforms — reliable without needing technical configuration
  • No-logs policy audited by KPMG (2025) — independent third-party verification
  • Trusted Server technology: servers run on RAM only, no data stored after power cycle
  • Unlocks most major streaming platforms including Netflix, Disney+, BBC iPlayer, and Amazon Prime

What doesn't

  • One of the more expensive mainstream VPNs — NordVPN and Surfshark both offer comparable performance at lower introductory rates
  • Maximum 8 simultaneous device connections — NordVPN allows 10, Surfshark is unlimited
  • Server count (undisclosed) is lower than NordVPN's 9,000+ and Surfshark's 4,500+
  • Owned by Kape Technologies since 2019 — a past acquisition that some privacy-focused users note when evaluating corporate structure
  • Fewer advanced features than NordVPN — no equivalent to Threat Protection Pro or Meshnet

Our Verdict

ExpressVPN earns an 8.8 — a genuinely capable VPN that earns its premium reputation on app quality, streaming reliability, and ease of use. For non-technical users and frequent streamers who want a VPN that just works without configuration, it's among the best. Where the score falls short of NordVPN's 9.1: the price gap has become harder to justify as NordVPN has closed the app-quality and reliability gap, and ExpressVPN's 8-device limit and lower server count are real trade-offs at its price point. If you prioritise the simplest possible experience and don't mind paying a premium, ExpressVPN is a confident recommendation. If you're optimising for price-to-performance, NordVPN or Surfshark offer stronger value on a 2-year plan.
