Pros & Cons

What works

  • Real-time grammar, spelling, and punctuation corrections across 500,000+ apps and websites
  • Tone detection and clarity suggestions help professionals tailor writing to any audience
  • Deep browser and OS integrations — works seamlessly in Gmail, Google Docs, Word, Slack, and more
  • GrammarlyGO delivers AI-powered rewrites, brainstorming, and drafts from a short prompt
  • Business plan includes shared style guides and brand tone settings for consistent team communication

What doesn't

  • Advanced suggestions — plagiarism detection, full-sentence rewrites, and tone transformation — are available on Pro and Business plans
  • Occasional over-suggestions on casual or creative writing where rule-bending is intentional
  • Primarily designed for English; multilingual writing support is limited
  • GrammarlyGO AI generation quality may vary on highly technical or niche subject matter

Our Verdict

Grammarly earns its 8.8 rating by delivering genuinely useful writing improvements at a scale no competitor matches — 500,000+ integrations means it works wherever you do, and its real-time feedback loop is fast enough to not break flow. The Pro plan is where the tool becomes most powerful, unlocking full AI rewrites, plagiarism detection, and advanced tone tools, making it a worthwhile investment for anyone who writes frequently in a professional context. For teams, the Business plan's shared style guides are a meaningful differentiator. Casual writers and those who work primarily in non-English languages may find the free tier covers their needs, while creative writers should expect to exercise judgment on suggestions that challenge stylistic choices.
