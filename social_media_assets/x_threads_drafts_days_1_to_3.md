**Post 2:**
Step 1: Pressure-test with Claude
Before writing code, ask Claude to play 3 roles: a skeptical investor, your target user, and a competitor. 
Find the sharpest objections. Don't build until you know exactly what assumption you are testing.

**Post 3:**
Step 2: Draft the Landing Page
Ask Claude to draft a 5-section page: headline, subheadline, one-line value prop, 3 benefits, and an email CTA. 
Iterate 3 times. The 3rd draft is usually the winner. Keep it short.

**Post 4:**
Step 3: Deploy on Hostinger
Get a clean .com or .io domain. Hostinger's shared hosting includes a free domain on annual plans (under $30/year). Use their drag-and-drop builder or drop in a single HTML file. Don't spend hours tweaking design.

**Post 5:**
Step 4: Wire up the Automation
Connect your email capture to n8n. Set up a webhook that triggers two things:
1. A personal welcome email to the user.
2. A Slack ping to you.
The goal isn't just signups—it's getting replies to that welcome email.
